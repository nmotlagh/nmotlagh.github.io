Modern adaptive QA systems have to choose among three actions — answer directly, retrieve and then answer, or abstain — but most work studies only one of these boundaries at a time. This paper asks a narrower question: under a fixed model–retriever–corpus stack, is the signal that says "retrieve" the same signal that says "abstain"?

The answer is no in the evaluated stack. Over 41,145 eval instances, logprob baselines top out at Recoverability AUC .518–.553 and have Reject Recall = 0 at their calibrated operating points. The strongest retriever-side scalar improves AUC but does not preserve useful reject behavior at the best-E2E threshold. A class-weighted question-only controller reaches Recoverability AUC .678 ± .005 with Reject Recall .487 ± .059, and at matched coverage improves Refine Recall over cumulative-logprob thresholds by +.252 [.233, .272].

Operationally, the 3-way routing problem is a two-stage control problem: answer confidence determines whether to leave direct answering, and stack-relative recoverability determines whether retrieval is likely to make the question answerable. The experiments identify class weighting as important for preserving non-trivial retrieve predictions when unrecoverable examples dominate the conditional dataset.
