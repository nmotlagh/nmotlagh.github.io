This manuscript is being prepared for ARR submission; the title and details are
withheld to preserve anonymous review. The work studies retrieval-augmented
selective QA: for each question, compare the model's direct answer with the
answer produced after retrieving evidence and revising, then measure whether
the revision helped, hurt, or made no difference.

The operating question is practical. Confidence estimates whether the draft
answer is already correct; a separate signal is needed to estimate whether
revising with retrieved evidence will improve it. The current manuscript reports
results on NQ-Open, TriviaQA, and PopQA. No acceptance or public paper link is
claimed here.
